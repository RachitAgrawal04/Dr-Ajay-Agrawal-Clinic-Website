Adolescent Mental Health: Early Warning Signs

Teen years are a major transition. Some ups and downs are normal—but certain changes can signal a deeper struggle. Here’s how to spot early signs and support your teen effectively.

Quick Summary

  • Watch for persistent changes in mood, sleep, appetite, or socializing
  • Note school decline, irritability, or loss of interest
  • Take self-harm talk seriously—seek help promptly
  • Respond with calm, validation, and professional support when needed

Early Warning Signs

  • Ongoing sadness, anxiety, irritability, or emotional swings
  • Withdrawing from friends/family; loss of interest in hobbies
  • Sleep changes: insomnia, very late nights, or oversleeping
  • Appetite/weight changes; low energy or frequent aches
  • Drop in grades, missing classes, or attention issues
  • Risky behavior, substance experimentation
  • Negative self-talk, hopelessness, or self-harm talk
  • Online behavior red flags: secrecy, bullying, unsafe challenges

How to Talk So Teens Feel Safe

  • Pick a calm time; start with curiosity, not conclusions
  • Use open questions: “I’ve noticed… How have you been feeling?”
  • Validate feelings first; advise later
  • Problem-solve together; agree on small next steps

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Symptoms persist for weeks or disrupt daily life
  • Self-harm thoughts or actions—seek urgent support
  • Substance misuse, eating disorders, or severe school decline

Early assessment can prevent escalation and support recovery.
